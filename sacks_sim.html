How It Works

IPL Sim is a cricket match simulator that uses real player statistics to simulate Twenty20 matches ball-by-ball. Every delivery is determined by a combination of batting ratings, bowling ratings, pitch conditions, match situation, and a touch of randomness.

SIMULATION ENGINE

Each ball resolves through a probability cascade: the engine first rolls for a wicket (scaled by bowler skill and pitch assist), then for a boundary (4 or 6), and finally a standard run (0–3). Phase multipliers shift these probabilities across powerplay, middle overs, and the death.

PITCH CONDITIONS

Green pitches boost pace/seam, dusty pitches assist spinners, dead tracks are batting paradises, and bouncy surfaces amplify pace. Dew in the second innings helps batters.

COLLAPSE PROTECTION

When multiple wickets fall quickly, the model dampens aggression to simulate the natural caution a new batsman applies — preventing unrealistic 10-wicket collapses in 3 overs.

PLAYER DATA

Ratings and strike rates are loaded live from players.json — the same data that powers your Discord bot. 267 players, real stats.
